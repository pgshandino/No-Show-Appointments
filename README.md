# No-Show Appointments: Brazilian Healthcare Patterns & Global Implications

## 📋 Overview

Appointments made should be honored by both parties involved. Unfortunately, that is not always the case. This analysis examines medical appointments with the aim of establishing patterns and trends to identify who is more likely to miss their appointment.

### 🌍 Cross-Cultural Context

**What does this Brazilian dataset tell us about patterns that likely apply to Nigerian healthcare?**

While this dataset originates from Brazil, the challenges and patterns identified here resonate across healthcare systems in developing nations, particularly in West Africa. Understanding no-show appointment behaviors can help healthcare providers globally optimize resource allocation and improve patient outcomes.

---

## 📊 Key Findings

### Overall No-Show Rate
- Approximately **20% of scheduled appointments result in no-shows**
- This represents significant waste in healthcare resources and scheduling inefficiency

### Demographic Patterns
- **Age**: Younger patients (< 18 years) and older patients (60+) show different no-show patterns
- **Gender**: Appointment adherence varies by gender, with implications for targeting interventions
- **Socioeconomic Factors**: Patients on government welfare assistance show higher no-show rates

### Medical & Behavioral Indicators
- **Comorbidities**: Patients with chronic diseases have better appointment adherence
- **Prior Behavior**: Patients with previous no-shows are more likely to miss future appointments
- **Wait Time**: Longer gaps between scheduling and appointment dates correlate with increased no-shows
- **Day of Week**: Appointments on specific days show variable attendance rates
- **Neighborhood**: Geographic and socioeconomic factors influence no-show likelihood

---

## 🎯 Actionable Insights

1. **SMS/Call Reminders**: Implement appointment reminders 24-48 hours before scheduled time
2. **Flexible Scheduling**: Offer appointment options that better align with patient availability
3. **Targeted Outreach**: Focus retention efforts on high-risk demographics
4. **Transportation Support**: Address logistical barriers, especially for lower-income patients
5. **Habit Formation**: Reward consistent appointment attendance with incentives
